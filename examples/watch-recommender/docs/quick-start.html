watch-rec Quick Start Guide

AI-powered movie and TV recommendations through natural conversation with ChatGPT or Claude.

Setup

You need two things: a connection to the MCP server and an auth token. Your auth token is provided when your account is created.

Claude (claude.ai)

  1. Click your profile icon (bottom-left) and select Settings
  2. Click Connectors in the sidebar
  3. Scroll down and click Add custom connector
  4. Enter the URL: https://scaffold-watch-rec.somotravel.workers.dev/?token=YOUR_AUTH_TOKEN
  5. Click Connect

Once connected, you'll see watch-rec listed under your connectors. Start a new chat and the tools are available immediately. You can also click the + button next to the chat input and select watch-rec to ensure it's used in that conversation.

Requires: Claude Pro, Max, Team, or Enterprise plan

ChatGPT

  1. Open Settings (gear icon)
  2. Go to Apps
  3. Click Advanced, then enable Developer Mode
  4. Click Create to add a new app
  5. Enter a name like watch-rec and a description like Movie and TV recommendations
  6. For Connector URL, enter: https://scaffold-watch-rec.somotravel.workers.dev/?token=YOUR_AUTH_TOKEN
  7. Uncheck the OAuth checkbox
  8. Click through the warning about custom connectors
  9. Save and close

You can also click the + button next to the chat input and select watch-rec to ensure it's used in that conversation.

Requires: ChatGPT Plus, Pro, Business, Enterprise, or Education plan

Claude Code / Claude Desktop

Add to your MCP config (~/.claude.json, claude_desktop_config.json, or project .mcp.json):

{
  "mcpServers": {
    "watch-rec": {
      "type": "url",
      "url": "https://scaffold-watch-rec.somotravel.workers.dev/mcp",
      "headers": {
        "Authorization": "Bearer YOUR_AUTH_TOKEN"
      }
    }
  }
}

Onboarding

Start a new conversation and say:

"Let's set up watch-rec"

The AI runs the onboarding flow, which walks you through four steps:

  1. Streaming services — which platforms you subscribe to (Netflix, Hulu, Max, etc.)
  2. Watch history import — upload a Netflix CSV export via the web UI, or skip this
  3. Taste discovery — rate 20 curated titles (1–5) so the system learns what you like
  4. Preference questions — answer a few follow-ups about your viewing habits

The whole process takes about 5 minutes. After onboarding, a taste profile is generated automatically.

You can redo this anytime by saying "Refine my taste profile".

Getting Recommendations

Once onboarded, just describe what you're in the mood for:

"I want something like Severance — smart, slow-burn, keeps you guessing"
"Recommend a comfort comedy for tonight"
"What should I watch with my kids this weekend?"

The AI will suggest 5–8 titles based on your taste profile, check them against your history (so you won't see repeats), and show where each one is streaming.

Managing Your Watchlist

Save titles for later, browse your queue, or change priorities.

What you sayWhat happens
"Add Zodiac to my watchlist"Saves it to your queue (medium priority)
"Add The Bear to my queue, high priority, tag it 'solo night'"Saves with priority and tags
"Show my watchlist"Lists everything sorted by priority
"Show my high priority queue"Filters by priority
"Remove Zodiac from my queue"Deletes it
"Change Zodiac to low priority"Updates priority

If a title is blocked because you've already watched or dismissed it, say:

"Add Zodiac to my queue, force it"

This clears the old state and re-adds it.

Logging What You've Watched

When you finish something, tell the AI:

"I just watched The Brutalist, I'd give it a 4 out of 5"
"Mark Nosferatu as watched"

This logs it to your history, removes it from your queue if it was there, and improves future recommendations.

Dismissing Titles

If you've already seen something or just aren't interested:

"I've already seen Oppenheimer"
"Not interested in that one"

Dismissed titles won't be recommended again.

Second Screen Mode

Watching something right now? Use the second screen feature for live companion info:

"I'm watching Severance S2E4"

You'll get:

  • Episode synopsis (spoiler-aware for current episode only)
  • Full cast and key crew
  • Character names and actors
  • Background context, filming locations, trivia

Use single-letter shortcuts to jump to specific info:

ShortcutWhat it shows
nNext interesting fact
cCast (actors and characters)
wWriters and directors
tTrivia
lFilming locations
hHistory and timeline

Examples:

"c" — show the cast
"t" — give me trivia
"n" — tell me something interesting

You can also ask freeform questions:

"Who plays Helly?"
"Tell me about the director"
"What else has that actor been in?"

This works for movies too:

"I'm watching There Will Be Blood"

Streaming Lookup

Check where any title is available:

"Where can I watch Conclave?"

Returns streaming (included with subscription), rental, and purchase options for your region.

Preferences

Your preferences shape recommendations. State them naturally:

"I love slow-burn thrillers but hate slasher horror"
"I prefer movies under 2 hours"
"Add HBO Max and Apple TV+ to my streaming services"

View everything stored:

"Show my preferences"

Taste Profile

Your taste profile is a summary of your viewing patterns, generated from your ratings and history. View it:

"Show my taste profile"

Regenerate it after logging more titles:

"Regenerate my taste profile"

Tips

Be specific about mood — "something like early Coen brothers" works better than "a good movie"
Rate titles during onboarding honestly — even rating things you disliked helps the system
Use tags on queue items — "date night", "solo", "family" make it easy to filter later
Force-add when correcting mistakes — if something was wrongly marked as watched, force-add it back to your queue
Use second screen for TV — include the season and episode number for episode-specific context
